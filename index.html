What are the parameters for the /schemeCreate cool color schemes! command?

Hue

Think of this as the base color. In the HSV color format, hue is the first value.

Type

What do you want the color pallete to look like?

Monochromatic- The monochromatic scheme is based on selecting a single hue on the color wheel, then adding more colors by varying the source color's saturation and brightness. Four colors will be produced.

Contrast- Contrast supplements the selected hue with its complement (the color opposite it on the color wheel) as another source color. 8 colors will be produced.

Triade- Triade takes the selected hue and adds two more source colors that are both a certain distance from the initial hue. 12 colors will be produced.

Tetrade- Tetrade is like Triade, but it adds another 2 source colors, creating 4 total sources. 16 colors will be produced.

Analogic- Analogic produces colors that are "analogous", or next to each other on the color wheel. 12 colors will be produced.

Variation

These variations will alter the produced colors. They basically work exactly like filters would in any image editing program.

Normal- No change in the colors.

Pastel- Produces pastel colors, which have in HSV high value and low-intermediate saturation.

Soft- Produces darker pastel colors.

Light- Very light, almost washed-out colors.

Hard- Deeper, very saturated colors.

Pale- Colors with more gray; less saturated.
